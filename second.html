how to write your cV ?

To write a CV that is optimized to pass ATS (ApplicantTrackingSoftware),which is commonly used by recruiters to extract information from candidates resumes and aid in the filtering process ,it is recommended to follow these best practices :

Summary: Start with a clear, concise and confident summary of your skills and experience, highlighting why you are the right candidate for the role.

Experience/Projects: List your work experience and key projects, focusing on achievements and quantifiable results that demonstrate your impact and value. Use keywords relevant to the job description to make it easier for ATS to match your CV to the role. If you are fresh and have no experience, you can include internships or other relevant experience, extracurricular activities, or academic achievements.

Education: Include your education history, including degrees, institutions, and graduation dates. Highlight relevant coursework and honors that demonstrate your skills and knowledge.

Skills: Make a list of your relevant skills and highlight any certifications or training that supports your qualifications. Use keywords from the job description to make it easier for ATS and recruiters to understand your skills and experience.






Some Tips To Write Good CV

  • Make Sure To Use keywords and phrases relevant to the job you are applying for in your CV, to ensure that the ATS software can easily identify your relevant skills and experience.

  • Use simple and straightforward language, avoiding using graphics or images that may not be recognized by the ATS.

  • Avoid using unconventional formatting or font styles, as these may also not be recognized by the ATS.

  • Use clear headings and bullet points to organize your CV, making it easy for the ATS and recruiters to quickly scan and understand your qualifications.

  • Ensure that all your contact information is up-to-date and clearly displayed on the CV.
